Rarely Available Landmark Lofts Penthouse Unit 401 With Personal Terrace

This fully renovated two-level residence at historic Landmark Lofts delivers 2,245 sq ft of interior living space plus an approximately 600 sq ft private terrace — one of the largest in the building — with 2 bedrooms, 2.5 baths, and 2 assigned garage parking spaces, steps from Union Station and the Capitol.

The main level opens to a sweeping living and dining space defined by exposed brick walls, freshly refinished hardwood floors, original industrial ductwork, a fireplace with stone surround, arched windows, built-in shelving, and a dramatic steel staircase. The kitchen features dark walnut cabinetry with glass uppers, black granite counters, brick backsplash, professional range, stainless hood, Sub-Zero refrigerator, and a breakfast bar open to the living area. A private office with full built-ins, a second bedroom, and two fully renovated baths — all new as of January 2026 — plus a powder room with floating vanity complete the main level.

Upstairs, the primary suite features original exposed timber beams spanning a vaulted ceiling above Brazilian cherry hardwood floors, an arched window, and a loft sitting area. The primary bath was fully gut-renovated with large-format marble tile floor and surround, soaking tub, frameless glass rain shower with rain head, floating double vanity, and dual LED-backlit mirrors.

The private terrace wraps the exterior of the penthouse level with direct French door access, multiple seating zones, and city views. At approximately 600 sq ft, it accommodates both a full dining setup and a separate lounge area — a rare find.

Major systems and finishes are all recently updated: new HVAC (2024), all new lighting and switches (2025), all bathrooms new (2026), floors recently refinished, fresh paint throughout, plus new washer/dryer and dishwasher (2022).

Landmark Lofts offers 24-hour concierge, rooftop pool, fitness center, theatre room, club room, and dry cleaning pickup. Whole Foods, Harris Teeter, Trader Joe's, Union Market, and the Red Line all within blocks.
